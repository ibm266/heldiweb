# Heldi brand voice

## One-line positioning

**Desi protein for Indian food:** protein that disappears into dal, curry, raita and the home-cooked meals you already love.

## Personality

| We are | We are not |
|--------|------------|
| Warm, playful, culturally specific | Clinical, gym-bro, Silicon Valley SaaS |
| Confident but inviting | Hype-y or aggressive |
| Family-table centred | Individual fitness obsession |
| Honest about food culture | Preachy about "clean eating" |
| Interactive and alive | Static corporate brochure |

Think: **your nani's kitchen meets a well-designed DTC brand**, Desi household references (nani, masala dabba, thali, mama choosing gold vs silver) without stereotype or caricature.

## Audience

- Primary: UK-based South Asian households cooking Indian food at home
- Secondary: Anyone who wants protein without changing what they eat
- Whole-family product: "Built for you. Made for the whole family."

## Vocabulary

### Always use

- **Stir**, **spoon**, **spoonful**; never "scoop"
- **Dal**, **curry**, **raita**, **dahi**, **chaat**: specific dishes, not "curries and sides"
- **Disappear into**, **vanish into**, **blend clean**: product behaviour
- **Desi**, **home-cooked**, **tonight's table**, **the whole table**
- **Heldi / Heldier**: brand as adjective: "The same food, just a little Heldier."
- **One pouch**: single SKU; one blend for everything gravy/dal/yoghurt-based

### Never use

- Scoop, shake, blender, shaker bottle, gym, gains, macros (unless quoting stats)
- Em dashes in copy
- Multiple SKUs, flavours, or "Chai/Khana/Dahi" line (retired)
- Generic "superfood" or wellness jargon without grounding in real meals
- "Healthy alternative" framing that implies the food they love isn't good enough

## Tone by context

### Headlines (Rozha One energy)

- Short, declarative, sometimes rhythmic: "No shaking. No blending. Just stir in."
- Benefit-led: "The food you love with the nutrients you need."
- Invitation: "Be first to stir it in."

### Body (Gelasio, conversational, precise)

- Explain whey in plain language (paneer liquid, filtered, dried)
- Reassure on taste: no chalky film, no protein-shake aftertaste
- Practical how-to: after cooking, into the bowl or whole pot, splash of water if needed

### Eyebrows (uppercase, spaced)

- Section labels: `THE POUCH`, `PUT IT ON THE TABLE`, `WITH YOUR FIRST ORDER`
- Terracotta on light; gold on ink sections

### Emotional lines (italic Gelasio)

- "/hel-dee/, adj. how my nani says 'healthy.'"
- "Never drink another protein shake again."
- "One email the day we launch, and a free jar with your first order."

## Key messages (approved)

1. **Same recipes. Same taste. More protein.** (packaging line)
2. **The same food, just a little Heldier.**
3. **Bringing something new to the table** (hero film tagline)
4. **Made in the UK · For Indian kitchens**
5. **No shaker, no blender · 100% vegetarian**
6. **Launching autumn 2026**

## Product facts (use consistently)

- 10g protein per bowl (placeholder, confirm before public claims)
- 90%+ whey protein isolate from British dairy
- 99% lactose-free
- No added sugar · Gluten free · 100% vegetarian
- Works in anything with gravy, dal, or yoghurt base (sambar, kadhi, korma, etc.)

## Cultural sensitivity

- Celebrate Indian home cooking as the hero, Heldi supports it, doesn't replace it
- Family hierarchy references (mama, nani, grandparents) should feel affectionate, not patronising
- Food photography and scenes should feel like real UK Desi homes, not stock "exotic India" tropes
- Vegetarian positioning matters, explain whey as paneer by-product when asked

## Copy patterns for graphics

**Social / ad headline formula:**

`[Desi food noun] + [protein benefit] + [no shake implied]`

Examples:
- "Desi protein that disappears into dal, curry, raita."
- "Stir it in. Eat what you love."
- "One pouch. The whole table."

**Stat callout formula:**

`[Number in terracotta Rozha One] + [short Gelasio descriptor]`

Example: **10g** protein per bowl

**CTA formula:**

- "Join waitlist" (primary)
- "How it works" (secondary outline)
- "Be first to stir it in." (closing section)
