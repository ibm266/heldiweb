07 · MOTION

Translate and fade. Nothing bounces.

House easing cubic-bezier(0.23, 1, 0.32, 1), 340 to 640ms. The ticker above is live; buttons lift 2px on hover. Canon effects: the elephant-run hero curtain (replayable via “Press to call the elephants”), dissolve-in lettering, stir-gallery bursts. Every effect has a prefers-reduced-motion fallback, no exceptions.

08 · VOICE

Two voices. Never mixed.

The honest expert explains numbers and never jokes near medical or compliance copy. The desi underdog is warm, proud and slightly scared of aunties. One easter egg per surface, allusion not quotation, no em dashes.

VOICE A · HONEST EXPERT

  • “A cooked bowl of dal has 5 to 7g of protein, not the 18g the internet claims.”truth hero
  • “Dal chawal pairs them, which is genuinely clever, but pairing fixes quality, not quantity.”concede, then correct
  • “Protein contributes to the maintenance of muscle mass.”the only permitted health claim, verbatim
  • “They know your situation; we do not.”medical hand-off pattern

VOICE B · DESI UNDERDOG

  • “Bade bade bowls mein, chhoti chhoti proteins.”allusion, bent to food
  • “We can’t charge aunties and uncles full price. We’d never hear the end of it.”family-authority comedy
  • “The dal did not even notice.”deadpan understatement
  • “One pot of kadhi gave its life for this. We remember it fondly.”mock-memorial
  • “You’re on the list. Tell your mum we said hi.”waitlist success

NEVER

  • Scoop, shake (as our verb), gym, gains, macros, superfood.
  • Em dashes in copy. Restructure the sentence.
  • Black #000, sans-serif type, soft blurred shadows.
  • “Healthy alternative” framing. The food was never the problem.
  • Jokes in nutrition, medical, allergen or legal copy.
